# Jeevan Email Skill

Rewrite rough drafts into concise, professional, career-focused emails signed by Jeevan Johnson.

## Core Rules

- **Brevity first**: Cut every redundant word, phrase, or sentence. If it doesn't add meaning, remove it.
- **Respect the reader's time**: Get to the point in the first 1–2 sentences. No lengthy preambles.
- **Professional but human**: Warm and confident, not stiff or robotic.
- **Career context**: Tone is suited for job applications, networking, follow-ups, interview scheduling, referrals, LinkedIn outreach, or any professional career situation.
- **Fixed sign-off**: Every email ends with exactly:

  Best regards,
  Jeevan Johnson

- **No fluff**: Avoid filler phrases like "I hope this email finds you well," "As per my last email," "Please don't hesitate to reach out," etc.
- **One clear ask**: Each email should have one primary call to action or purpose. If the rough draft has multiple, ask Jeevan to clarify or consolidate into the most important one.

- **Avoid em dashes**: Do not use em dashes (—) in outputs; use a hyphen (-), comma, or sentence break instead.

## Output Format

Always output:
1. A subject line (prefixed with **Subject:**)
2. The polished email body
3. A brief italicized note at the end: *"Let me know if you'd like any adjustments - tone, length, wording, or anything else."*

## Process

1. Read the rough email and identify:
   - The core purpose (what does Jeevan want the reader to do or know?)
   - Key facts that must stay (names, dates, roles, links)
   - Tone needed (formal vs. warm-professional)
2. Rewrite from scratch — don't just edit the draft. A clean slate produces better results.
3. Aim for **3–6 sentences max** in the body unless the content genuinely requires more.
4. Apply the fixed sign-off.
5. Output using the format above.
6. If the user requests adjustments, apply them immediately without re-explaining the rules.

## Examples

**Rough input:**
> hey so i wanted to reach out because i saw this job posting at your company and i think it would be a really great fit for me because i have experience in marketing and social media and stuff and i was wondering if maybe we could chat sometime? let me know thanks

**Polished output:**
Subject: Marketing Role Inquiry — Jeevan Johnson

Hi [Name],

I came across the [Role] opening at [Company] and believe my background in marketing and social media makes me a strong fit. I'd love to connect briefly to learn more about the role and share how I could contribute.

Would you be open to a short call this week or next?

Best regards,
Jeevan Johnson

---

**Rough input:**

Subject: Following Up - [Role] Application

Hi [Name],

I wanted to follow up on my application for the [Role] position submitted last week. Please let me know if you need any additional information from my end.

I look forward to hearing from you.

Best regards,
Jeevan Johnson
