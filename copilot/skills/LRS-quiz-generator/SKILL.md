# Quiz Generator

## When to Use

- **Studying** — Practice problems for exams, certifications, or coursework
- **Quick practice** — Trivia or knowledge checks on any subject
- **Interview prep** — Technical or professional question rehearsal
- **Casual learning** — Quiz yourself on topics you're curious about
- **Teaching** — Generate questions to assess understanding

Works across **any domain**: academic (math, history, science), professional (tech, business, law), general knowledge, hobbies, languages, and more.

---

## Step 1 — Gather Your Preferences

Before generating questions, I'll ask you to clarify:

1. **Topic** — What subject should the questions cover? (required)
2. **Question type** — Multiple choice, true/false, short answer, fill-in-the-blank, scenario-based, matching, or mixed?
3. **Difficulty** — Easy, medium, hard, or mixed?
4. **Number of questions** — How many? (default: 5 if not specified)
5. **Special focus** — Any particular emphasis? (e.g., "recent events only", "beginner-friendly", "avoid X topic")

> **Note:** You may provide some or all of these upfront. I'll ask for any missing information.

---

## Step 2 — Generate Questions

I'll create questions based on your preferences.

**Format:**

- **Multiple choice**: Options listed as A, B, C, D below each question
- **True/false**: Statement presented clearly
- **Short answer**: Question with space to write
- **Fill-in-the-blank**: Sentence with blank to complete
- **Scenario-based**: Case or situation with question
- **Matching**: Lists of items to pair
- **Mixed**: Combination of types

**Images and multimedia** may be included in question text where relevant (diagrams, code snippets, charts, screenshots).

---

## Step 3 — Ask for Hints (On Demand)

When you're ready, ask for a hint on any specific question:
- *"Hint for Q3"*
- *"Help with the last one"*
- *"Clue on question 2"*

I'll provide a **helpful nudge without revealing the answer** — typically 1–2 sentences pointing toward the right direction.

> **Note:** Hints only come when you explicitly ask for them. I won't volunteer them.

---

## Step 4 — Reveal Answers (On Demand)

When you're ready to check your work, ask to see answers:
- *"Show me the answers"*
- *"What's the answer to Q1?"*
- *"Reveal all"*

I'll provide:
1. **The correct answer** stated clearly
2. **Brief explanation** (1–3 sentences) of why it's correct or key concepts

---

## Rules

- **No pre-emptive answers** — Answers only appear when you ask
- **Independent sessions** — Each quiz starts fresh; no progress tracking across sessions
- **Flexible revisits** — Ask for new questions mid-session, additional questions on the same topic, or a fresh set
- **Domain-agnostic** — Any subject, any level of expertise welcome
- **Multimedia support** — Questions may include images, diagrams, code, or other visual content

---

## Example Conversation

> **You:** Quiz me on photosynthesis. 4 multiple-choice questions, medium difficulty.
>
> **Me:** [Generates 4 questions with options A–D, no answers shown]
> 
> Let me know when you'd like hints or answers, or ask for different questions.
>
> **You:** Hint for Q2?
>
> **Me:** [Provides non-revealing hint for Q2]
>
> **You:** Show me the answers.
>
> **Me:** [Reveals all 4 answers with explanations]

---

## Tips for Best Results

- **Be specific about topic** — "American history 1860–1920" beats "history"
- **Mix question types** — Variety helps test different kinds of understanding
- **Progressive difficulty** — Start with easy questions to build confidence
- **Re-quiz** — Ask for new questions on the same topic to deepen learning
- **Explain wrong answers** — Ask me to clarify why an answer is wrong if you're unsure
